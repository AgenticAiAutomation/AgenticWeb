Power Automate FAQs.

Power Automate (formerly Microsoft Flow) is Microsoft's workflow automation platform included in most Microsoft 365 plans. It connects 400+ apps and services through pre-built connectors, allowing you to automate repetitive tasks across Outlook, Teams, SharePoint, Dynamics, Excel, and external services — without writing code. For businesses already on Microsoft 365, it's the most cost-effective automation tool available.
Yes — Power Automate is included in most Microsoft 365 Business and Enterprise plans, giving you access to standard connectors and a generous flow run allowance. Premium connectors (for Salesforce, Dynamics, SAP, etc.) require an additional Power Automate per-user licence (approx ₹1,200/user/month). Most of our implementations use only standard connectors — meaning no additional licence cost for you.
Yes. Power Automate has 400+ built-in connectors including Salesforce, HubSpot, Google Workspace, Slack, Shopify, Razorpay, SQL databases, and REST APIs. For systems without a connector, we use the HTTP/webhook connector to build custom integrations. This means Power Automate can be the central hub connecting your Microsoft stack with any third-party business tool.
Power Automate is the right choice if your team already works in Microsoft 365 — it integrates deeply with Teams, SharePoint, and Dynamics in ways that external tools can't match. n8n is better for businesses outside the Microsoft ecosystem, for complex custom logic, or for self-hosted deployments where data sovereignty is critical. Many clients use both: Power Automate for internal M365 workflows, n8n for external system integrations.
